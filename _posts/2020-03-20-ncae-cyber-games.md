Back in February, I participated with my collegiate CTF team in the NCAE Cyber Games. Our CTF group isn't really a set number or fixed member group. Most of the time its just people who feel up for the challenge that week. This was one that we got together basically last minute and we ended up getting placed in the wildcard category for the competition. The competition was similar to CCDC but was more beginner friendly as well as had a CTF component. For those who don't know what CCDC, the premise is that during the competition red teamers (i.e. hackers) would be trying to crash our systems using footholds or malicious scripts that they had previously planted in our virtual network. Our job is to defend the network by keeping our systems up and kicking them our of their footholds. When our systems are up, we get points. 

But back to the competition. Though we were a ragtag team, we ended up taking first in this part of the competition. The fight was hard but we worked as a team and secured the win. The next part of the competition would be in a couple of weeks so this time we had an opportunity to prepare a defense plan. A couple days before the competition, our team got together and put together our game plan. The plan consisted of locking down and open ports besides the necessary ones, changing default ports, looking for uncommon or unexpected files in our system, and checking for any suspicious users.

When the competition started, we did exactly that. Luckily most of that kept the red team at bay for a while. Slowly but surely they worked their way back in to our systems and kept a lot of our main services down. By the end of the competition, we were able to keep most of our systems up and, supplemented by the points from the CTF challenges, were able to take 2nd. This part of the competition was harder than the previous and challenged my teams knowledge and experience. But throughout it all, it was a great experience and we hope to participate again next year.

Personally, CCDC and networking in general have been confusing to me and have never been my strong suit. This is something I am hoping to change this next Fall as I go into Junior year. Junior year is all about networking and databases which are things I haven't had a lot of experience with. I undertook a personal project to learn more about networking which is what this site and my whole domain have helped me do. Previously, I knew nothing concerning DNS, Proxies, or even web hosting. I still dont know everything but I think what I have learned here will help lay the foundation for my learning this next fall. If you made it here, thanks for reading and keep on hacking!
